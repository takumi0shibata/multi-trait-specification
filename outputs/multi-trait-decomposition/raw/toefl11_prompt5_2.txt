### Coherence and Cohesion

Coherence and cohesion refer to the clarity and logical flow of ideas in a text, ensuring that information is presented in an organized manner that is easy for the reader to follow.

### Score Intervals and Characteristics

- **Score 0-1**: The text fails to communicate any message or has no discernible organization, making it incomprehensible to the reader.

- **Score 2-3**: The text exhibits very little control over organizational features, with a lack of logical structure and minimal use of cohesive devices, resulting in confusion.

- **Score 4-5**: The text presents information but lacks coherent arrangement and clear progression; cohesive devices may be used inaccurately or repetitively, and paragraphing may be inadequate.

- **Score 6-7**: The text is arranged coherently with a clear overall progression; cohesive devices are used effectively, though there may be occasional faults in cohesion or referencing, and paragraphing is generally logical.

- **Score 8-9**: The text is skillfully organized with logical sequencing of ideas; cohesion is managed seamlessly, and paragraphing is used appropriately to enhance clarity and flow.
