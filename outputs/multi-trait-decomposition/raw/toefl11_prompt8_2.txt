### Coherence and Cohesion
Coherence and cohesion refer to the logical organization of ideas and the effective use of linguistic devices that connect sentences and paragraphs, ensuring clarity and flow in writing.

### Scoring Intervals

**Score Range: 0-2**
- **Characteristics:** Very poor organization with little to no logical flow; fails to communicate a clear message or ideas effectively.

**Score Range: 3-4**
- **Characteristics:** Limited organization of ideas; basic cohesive devices may be used inaccurately or repetitively, leading to confusion in the overall structure.

**Score Range: 5-6**
- **Characteristics:** Some organization present, but lacks overall progression; cohesive devices are used, though they may be mechanical or inconsistent, and paragraphing may not always be logical.

**Score Range: 7-8**
- **Characteristics:** Information is logically organized with clear progression; a range of cohesive devices is used appropriately, and paragraphing is sufficient and appropriate.

**Score Range: 9-10**
- **Characteristics:** Exceptional organization and flow; cohesion is seamless and unobtrusive, with skillful paragraphing that enhances the clarity and effectiveness of the writing.
