### Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Grammatical Range and Accuracy refers to the variety and correctness of grammatical structures used in writing or speaking, assessing how well a person can communicate their ideas without errors.

### Score Intervals

- **Score 0-2**: 
  - Limited or no ability to use sentence forms; communication is severely hindered, often relying on memorized phrases or completely lacking structure.

- **Score 3-4**: 
  - Attempts to use sentence forms but frequent grammatical and punctuation errors distort meaning; very limited range of structures with rare use of complex sentences.

- **Score 5-6**: 
  - Mixes simple and complex sentences; some grammatical errors are present but do not significantly impede communication; attempts at complexity may be less accurate.

- **Score 7-8**: 
  - Demonstrates a good variety of complex structures with frequent error-free sentences; occasional minor errors may occur but do not detract from overall clarity.

- **Score 9-10**: 
  - Exhibits a wide range of structures with full flexibility and accuracy; errors are rare and typically minor, showcasing a high level of grammatical control.
