### Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Grammatical Range and Accuracy refers to the variety and correctness of sentence structures used in writing or speaking, reflecting the speaker's or writer's command of grammar.

### Score Intervals

- **Score 0-2**: 
  - Characterized by an inability to use sentence forms effectively, with reliance on memorized phrases or complete lack of grammatical structure.

- **Score 3-4**: 
  - Demonstrates attempts at sentence formation, but frequent grammatical and punctuation errors distort meaning, with limited use of complex structures.

- **Score 5-6**: 
  - Shows a mix of simple and complex sentences, with some grammatical errors that may occasionally hinder communication, but generally maintains a basic level of accuracy.

- **Score 7-8**: 
  - Utilizes a variety of complex structures with good control over grammar and punctuation, producing mostly error-free sentences with only occasional minor mistakes.

- **Score 9-10**: 
  - Exhibits a wide range of structures with full flexibility and accuracy, making rare minor errors that do not detract from overall communication.
