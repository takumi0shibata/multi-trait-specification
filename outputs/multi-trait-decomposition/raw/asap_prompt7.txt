**Essay Scoring Rubric**

**Dimension 1: Ideas (Score doubled)**  
This dimension evaluates the focus and development of ideas in the story. A high score indicates that the story is clearly focused on the topic of patience and is thoroughly developed with specific, relevant details.

- **Score 10:** The story is exceptionally focused on the topic of patience and is thoroughly developed with specific, relevant details that enhance the narrative.
- **Score 8:** The story is clearly focused on the topic of patience and is well-developed with relevant details.
- **Score 6:** The story is somewhat focused on the topic of patience and is developed with a mix of specific and general details.
- **Score 4:** The story is minimally focused on the topic of patience and is developed with limited and/or general details.
- **Score 2:** The story lacks focus on the topic of patience and is underdeveloped.
- **Score 0:** The story is not focused on the topic of patience and lacks development.

**Dimension 2: Organization**  
This dimension assesses the clarity and logical sequencing of the organization and connections between ideas and/or events. A high score indicates a well-organized story with clear and logical progression.

- **Score 10:** The organization and connections between ideas and/or events are exceptionally clear and logically sequenced, enhancing the narrative flow.
- **Score 8:** The organization and connections between ideas and/or events are clear and logically sequenced.
- **Score 6:** The organization and connections between ideas and/or events are logically sequenced.
- **Score 4:** The organization and connections between ideas and/or events are weak.
- **Score 2:** The organization and connections between ideas and/or events are unclear.
- **Score 0:** No organization is evident.

**Dimension 3: Style**  
This dimension evaluates the command of language, including word choice and sentence structure. A high score indicates effective and compelling language use that supports the writer's purpose and audience.

- **Score 10:** The command of language, including effective and compelling word choice and varied sentence structure, clearly supports the writer's purpose and audience.
- **Score 8:** The command of language, including effective word choice and varied sentence structure, supports the writer's purpose and audience.
- **Score 6:** Adequate command of language, including effective word choice and clear sentences, supports the writer's purpose and audience.
- **Score 4:** Limited use of language, including lack of variety in word choice and sentences, may hinder support for the writer's purpose and audience.
- **Score 2:** Ineffective use of language that does not support the writer's purpose and audience.
- **Score 0:** Language use is ineffective and does not support the writer's purpose and audience.

**Dimension 4: Conventions**  
This dimension assesses the use of conventions of Standard English for grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation. A high score indicates consistent and appropriate use of conventions.

- **Score 10:** Consistent, appropriate use of conventions of Standard English for grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation.
- **Score 8:** Mostly consistent, appropriate use of conventions of Standard English for grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation.
- **Score 6:** Adequate use of conventions of Standard English for grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation.
- **Score 4:** Limited use of conventions of Standard English for grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation.
- **Score 2:** Ineffective use of conventions of Standard English for grammar, usage, spelling, capitalization, and punctuation.
- **Score 0:** Conventions of Standard English are not used effectively.
