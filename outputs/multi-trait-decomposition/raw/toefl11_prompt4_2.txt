### Coherence and Cohesion

Coherence and cohesion refer to the logical organization of ideas and the effective use of linguistic devices to connect sentences and paragraphs, ensuring clarity and flow in writing.

### Scoring Intervals

**Score Range: 0-10**

1. **Score: 0-1**
   - The writing fails to communicate any message or lacks any organizational features, making it incomprehensible.

2. **Score: 2-3**
   - The writing shows very little control over organizational features, with a lack of logical structure and minimal use of cohesive devices.

3. **Score: 4-5**
   - The writing presents information but lacks coherent arrangement and clear progression; cohesive devices may be used inaccurately or repetitively, and paragraphing may be inadequate.

4. **Score: 6-7**
   - The writing is organized coherently with a clear progression of ideas; cohesive devices are used effectively, though there may be occasional faults in cohesion or referencing, and paragraphing is present but may not always be logical.

5. **Score: 8-9**
   - The writing is skillfully organized with logical sequencing of information and ideas; cohesion is managed well, attracting no attention, and paragraphing is used appropriately to enhance clarity and flow.
