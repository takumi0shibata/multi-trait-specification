### Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Grammatical Range and Accuracy refers to the variety and correctness of grammatical structures used in writing or speaking, assessing how well a person can communicate their ideas without errors.

### Score Intervals

**Score 0-2: Limited Ability**
- **Characteristics:** The individual struggles to use sentence forms, relying on memorized phrases or producing very few coherent sentences. Errors are frequent and severely hinder communication.

**Score 3-4: Basic Control**
- **Characteristics:** The individual attempts to use sentence forms but makes numerous grammatical and punctuation errors that distort meaning. There is a very limited range of structures, with rare use of complex sentences.

**Score 5-6: Moderate Proficiency**
- **Characteristics:** The individual uses a mix of simple and complex sentences, with some errors in grammar and punctuation. While errors may occur, they do not significantly impede understanding.

**Score 7-8: Good Command**
- **Characteristics:** The individual demonstrates a variety of complex structures and produces many error-free sentences. There may be occasional minor errors, but overall, grammar and punctuation are well controlled.

**Score 9-10: Excellent Mastery**
- **Characteristics:** The individual uses a wide range of grammatical structures with full flexibility and accuracy. Errors are rare and typically only minor slips, indicating a high level of proficiency in grammar.
