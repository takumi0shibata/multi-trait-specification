### Coherence and Cohesion

Coherence and cohesion refer to the logical organization of ideas and the effective use of linguistic devices that connect sentences and paragraphs, ensuring clarity and flow in writing.

### Scoring Intervals

**Score Range: 0-10**

1. **Score: 0-1**
   - The writing fails to communicate any message or lacks any organizational features, making it incomprehensible.

2. **Score: 2-3**
   - The text shows very little control over organizational features, with a lack of logical structure and minimal use of cohesive devices, resulting in unclear communication.

3. **Score: 4-5**
   - Information is presented with some organization, but there is a noticeable lack of overall progression. Cohesive devices may be used inaccurately or repetitively, and paragraphing may be inadequate.

4. **Score: 6-7**
   - Ideas are arranged coherently with a clear overall progression. Cohesive devices are used effectively, though there may be occasional faults. Paragraphing is present but may not always be logical.

5. **Score: 8-9**
   - The writing is logically organized with a clear progression of ideas. Cohesion is managed skillfully, attracting no attention, and paragraphing is used appropriately to enhance clarity and flow.
