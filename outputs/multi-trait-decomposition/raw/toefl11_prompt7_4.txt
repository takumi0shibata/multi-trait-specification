### Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Grammatical Range and Accuracy refers to the variety and correctness of grammatical structures used in writing or speaking, assessing how well a person can communicate their ideas without errors.

### Score Intervals

- **Score 0-2**: 
  - Limited or no ability to use sentence forms; communication is severely hindered, relying on memorized phrases or completely lacking structure.

- **Score 3-4**: 
  - Attempts to use sentence forms but frequent grammatical errors distort meaning; very limited range of structures with predominant inaccuracies.

- **Score 5-6**: 
  - Mix of simple and complex sentences; some grammatical errors present, but they rarely impede understanding; attempts at complexity are evident but not always accurate.

- **Score 7-8**: 
  - Good control of a variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences with only occasional minor errors or inappropriacies; demonstrates a solid grasp of grammar and punctuation.

- **Score 9-10**: 
  - Exceptional use of a wide range of structures with full flexibility and accuracy; errors are rare and typically minor, showcasing a high level of grammatical proficiency.
