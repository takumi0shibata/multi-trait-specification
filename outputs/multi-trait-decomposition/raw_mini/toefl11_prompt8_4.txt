### Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Grammatical Range and Accuracy refers to the variety and correctness of grammatical structures used in writing or speaking, assessing how well a person can communicate their ideas without errors.

### Score Intervals

- **Score 0-2**: 
  - Limited or no ability to use sentence forms; communication is severely hindered, often relying on memorized phrases or completely lacking structure.

- **Score 3-4**: 
  - Attempts to use sentence forms but frequent grammatical and punctuation errors distort meaning; very limited range of structures with rare use of complex sentences.

- **Score 5-6**: 
  - Mix of simple and complex sentences with some grammatical errors; while errors may occur, they rarely impede overall communication; a moderate range of structures is used.

- **Score 7-8**: 
  - Good control of a variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences with only occasional minor errors or inappropriacies; demonstrates a strong command of grammar.

- **Score 9-10**: 
  - Exceptional use of a wide range of structures with full flexibility and accuracy; errors are rare and typically minor, showcasing a high level of grammatical proficiency.
