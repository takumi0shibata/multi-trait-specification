### Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Grammatical Range and Accuracy refers to the variety and correctness of grammatical structures used in writing or speaking, assessing how effectively a person can communicate their ideas without errors.

### Score Intervals

- **Score 0-1**: 
  - No ability to use sentence forms; communication is not possible.

- **Score 2-3**: 
  - Limited to memorized phrases; attempts at sentence construction are frequent but heavily flawed, leading to distorted meaning.

- **Score 4-5**: 
  - Uses a very limited range of structures; attempts at complex sentences are common but often inaccurate, resulting in frequent grammatical errors that can hinder understanding.

- **Score 6-7**: 
  - Employs a mix of simple and complex sentences; while some errors in grammar and punctuation exist, they are infrequent and do not significantly impede communication.

- **Score 8-9**: 
  - Demonstrates a wide range of grammatical structures with high accuracy; errors are rare and typically minor, showcasing strong control over language use.
